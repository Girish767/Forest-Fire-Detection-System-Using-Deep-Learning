About FireGuard AI

Our mission is to leverage cutting-edge artificial intelligence to protect our forests, wildlife, and communities from the devastating impact of wildfires.

The Technology

FireGuard AI is built upon the SigLIP (Sigmoid Loss for Language Image Pre-training) architecture developed by Google. Unlike traditional Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), SigLIP uses a Vision Transformer (ViT) approach, allowing it to understand global context within an image—crucial for distinguishing between smoke, clouds, and fog.

The model processes images in patches, analyzing the relationship between different parts of the frame to detect subtle visual cues of early-stage fires.

The Dataset

Our model was fine-tuned on a diverse dataset comprising thousands of images from various sources, ensuring robustness across different environments and lighting conditions.

Data Sources:

  • FLAME Dataset: Aerial imagery of pile burns and prescribed fires.
  • DeepFire: A large-scale dataset for forest fire detection.
  • Custom Collection: Synthetic smoke and fire images for edge-case training.
